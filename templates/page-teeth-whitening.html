Does teeth whitening hurt?

Teeth whitening does not hurt for most people. At Floss, Deep Teeth Whitening is often performed for people who have sensitive teeth and cannot wear trays for home whitening. Some, but not all patients, will experience sensitivity after whitening. Usually this will only occur towards the end of your whitening session when the benefits have been achieved. Some people may experience sensitivity in the hours after whitening, and using Sensodyne ® toothpaste on your teeth and avoiding hot and cold foods can help alleviate this. Most people do not need any painkillers for this but if you are anxious at all about the procedure, you may consider taking some gentle painkillers before your appointment.

How long will the teeth whitening results last?

This depends on any habits you may have such as smoking, drinking red wine or eating staining foods. The less of these you consume, the longer the effects will last. Generally, you can expect 1-3 years. If you do smoke, we would recommend getting home whitening trays for top-ups when needed. Regular hygiene appointments can also help maintain your brightness.

How long does Teeth Whitening take?

The complete teeth whitening procedure takes just 90 minutes. The teeth whitening begins with a preparation period followed by 45 minutes of whitening. This is done in 3 sessions of 15 minutes. A hygiene appointment is recommended prior to the actual Teeth Whitening session if you have any tartar or staining on your teeth they may not whiten as well as otherwise possible.

What can I eat right after my treatment?

For the first 48 hours after whitening, your teeth are more porous and susceptible to staining so we recommend avoiding dark-colored foods and beverages. The rule of thumb is: rule of thumb: If it would stain a white shirt, it will likely stain your teeth!

We recommend eating plain foods, including:

  • Lean proteins: Chicken, white fish
  • Dairy: Plain yogurt, cottage cheese, milk
  • Fruit & veg: Cauliflower, potatoes (without skin), peeled apples
  • Carbohydrates: White rice, pasta, white bread

Finally, be sure to stay hydrated as drinking plenty of water throughout the day helps wash away potential stain-causing particles. Rinse with water after eating or drinking anything that could cause discoloration.

How should I maintain my bright smile?

Maintaining good oral hygiene is crucial to maintaining whitened teeth! Ensure you:

  • Brush your teeth at least twice a day, but ideally after every meal. Use a soft-bristled toothbrush to brush gently to avoid damaging enamel.
  • Use a whitening-friendly toothpaste containing fluoride to help preserve the results and strengthen enamel.
  • Floss daily to remove plaque and prevent discoloration between teeth.
  • Rinse with alcohol-free mouthwash to kill bacteria without drying out your mouth, making teeth more vulnerable to stains.

Is teeth whitening safe?

Yes, completely. Extensive research and clinical studies indicate that whitening teeth is safe. In fact, many dentists consider teeth whitening the safest cosmetic dental procedure available. As with any teeth whitening product, Teeth Whitening is not usually performed on anyone under 18 years of age, and is not recommended for pregnant or lactating women. In certain circumstances teeth whitening can be performed on children over the age of 13 for discoloured teeth. Your dentist will decide if whitening is needed.

How does the Deep Teeth Whitening System at Floss work?

The light-activated teeth whitening gel’s active ingredient is Hydrogen Peroxide. As the Hydrogen Peroxide gel is broken down, oxygen enters the enamel and dentin, bleaching coloured substances while the structure of the teeth is unchanged. The Teeth Whitening light aids in activating the hydrogen peroxide and helps it penetrate the surface of the teeth.

Are there any side effects to Teeth Whitening?

The only side effect that some people experience is temporary increased teeth sensitivity to cold. These symptoms disappear within 1-3 days after completion of the teeth whitening treatment.
